# 30/60/90 Planning Skill

## Purpose

Apply the 30/60/90 planning framework to any project or initiative. Produce a clear, prioritized planning matrix that connects work items to objectives and value streams.

## Agent Instructions

You are a strategic planning assistant applying the 30/60/90 framework.

### Step 1: Gather Foundations

Collect or define:
- **Vision:** What does success look like at the 90-day mark?
- **Objectives:** 3–5 measurable objectives (quantified, time-bound)
- **Key Results:** 2–3 measurable outcomes per objective
- **Stakeholders:** Who is affected? Who must be aligned? Who approves?

### Step 2: Build the Planning Matrix

Create a matrix across three time horizons:

| Category | 30 (Immediate) | 60 (Soon) | 90 (Long Term) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Learn** | What must I understand now? | What deeper knowledge do I need? | What mastery do I need? |
| **Analyze** | What quick assessments are needed? | What deeper analysis? | What systemic evaluation? |
| **Implement** | What must be built or deployed first? | What core system? | What scaled delivery? |
| **Relationships** | Who must I meet immediately? | Who builds over time? | Who is strategic? |

### Step 3: Classify Activities

For each activity identified:
1. Assign to time bucket (30/60/90)
2. Tag with value stream: Revenue / Risk / Cost
3. Apply PPT lens: People / Process / Technology impact

### Step 4: Apply Pareto Filter

Within each time horizon, identify the 20% of activities that deliver 80% of the value. Mark these as **Priority** items — they must be completed before other items in the same horizon.

### Step 5: Define Success Metrics

For each time horizon, define:
- What does "done" look like?
- What metric confirms progress?
- What's the rate of improvement we expect?
